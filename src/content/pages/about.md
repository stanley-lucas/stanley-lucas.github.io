# About

I’m Stanley Lucas, an Air Traffic Controller and Computer Engineer based in Brazil, focused on building reliable systems for complex operational environments.

My background combines aviation operations, software engineering, and academic research in physics and astrophysics — experiences that shaped my interest in aerospace systems, real-time infrastructure, and large-scale engineering problems.

Working in air traffic control gave me direct exposure to safety-critical operations where reliability, situational awareness, and decision-making matter every second. Over time, that environment pushed me deeper into software development and the engineering behind the systems that support modern aviation and aerospace operations.

Today, I build projects centered around:
- simulation systems
- telemetry and data pipelines
- backend infrastructure
- observability platforms
- real-time and distributed systems
- aerospace and air traffic control technology

My current work is less about building apps and more about understanding how to design systems that are robust, observable, and operationally reliable.

# Why this blog

This blog documents my transition from operational aviation into aerospace-focused engineering.

I write about:
- aerospace and aviation systems
- air traffic control technology
- software engineering and architecture
- telemetry, simulation, and observability
- engineering trade-offs and system design
- personal projects and technical experiments

Some posts are deep dives into systems I’m building. Others are reflections on engineering decisions, operational thinking, and technologies that I believe will shape the future of aerospace systems.

Writing is part of how I learn. It forces me to think more clearly, question assumptions, and document the reasoning behind the systems I build.
